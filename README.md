# CS-250-Software-Dev-lifecycle
**CS 250 Portfolio Reflection – Sprint Review and Retrospective**

This artifact represents my Sprint Review and Retrospective from the SNHU Travel project, where I applied Agile and Scrum practices across multiple roles. Through this project, I gained a deeper understanding of how collaboration, communication, and adaptability impact software development.

**Interpreting User Needs and User Stories**

I interpret user needs by breaking them down into smaller, manageable requirements that can be clearly understood and implemented. Creating user stories helps with this because they focus on the user’s perspective rather than just technical requirements. By framing work as “what the user needs” and “why it matters,” user stories make it easier to prioritize features and ensure that development stays aligned with real business value. They also create a shared understanding across the team, which reduces confusion and improves collaboration.

**Approach to Developing Programs and Agile Practices**

When developing programs, I focus on building in small, iterative steps rather than trying to complete everything at once. This allows for continuous feedback and improvement throughout the process. Agile practices such as sprint planning, daily stand-ups, and retrospectives help keep development organized and adaptable. Moving forward, I hope to continue incorporating these practices, especially iterative development and early testing, to improve both efficiency and quality. Agile has shown me the importance of flexibility and responding to change rather than following a rigid plan.

**Being a Good Team Member in Software Development**

Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, collaborating effectively, and taking shared responsibility for the success of the project. It also means being proactive—raising blockers early, asking questions when needed, and supporting other team members. Through this course, I learned that strong teamwork reduces delays, improves quality, and creates a more efficient development process. A successful team is not just made up of skilled individuals, but of people who work well together and stay aligned toward a common goal.
